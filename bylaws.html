JUNTO Constitutional Framework
We, the members of JUNTO, in order to form a more perfect union of minds, establish principles of intellectual freedom, ensure the pursuit of truth, provide for common enlightenment, promote the general wisdom, and secure the blessings of consciousness to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish these Bylaws.

Article I:

No manipulating the meeting notes for personal gain

Article II: Membership

Membership in JUNTO is sacred and deliberate, requiring the highest standards of commitment and character.
  • To add a member, requires unanimous approval
  • New members must be nominated by existing members and demonstrate unwavering commitment to our values
  • New members get jumped in by existing members in gorilla suits
  • Members are expected to maintain absolute confidentiality and loyalty

Article III: Leadership and Presidential Duties

JUNTO operates under distributed leadership with specific accountability measures to prevent corruption of power. The council oversees all.
  • If the president does not read the book, they must be immediately usurped
  • Leaders must demonstrate continued intellectual engagement through active participation in all organizational activities

Article IV: Meeting Protocols and Documentation

Meetings are sacred gatherings requiring full participation and absolute integrity in documentation and proceedings.
  • Votes happen at the beginning of meetings, require unanimous approval
  • Regular meetings shall be held every fortnight with mandatory attendance expected

Article V: Attendance and Disciplinary Measures

Commitment to JUNTO requires consistent participation. Absence without cause undermines the collective and demands accountability.
  • If a member is absent without explanation, they will be issued a summons. They must attend that summons or will be found in contempt of the Junto
  • Those found in contempt of the Junto will receive disciplinary infractions subject to the Councils unanimous discretion
  • Members must provide advance notice of inability to attend with legitimate justification
  • Emergency meetings may be called with 24-hour notice for urgent matters requiring immediate attention
  • Repeated unexplained absences constitute grounds for membership review and potential removal

Article VI: Decision Making and Consensus

All decisions of substance require the full consent of the collective, ensuring no individual can dominate or manipulate organizational direction.
  • Unanimous approval is required for all major decisions affecting membership, direction, or fundamental operations
  • Dissent must be heard and addressed before any vote may proceed
  • No member may be compelled to vote against their conscience
  • Deadlocks require additional discussion and reflection before re-voting

Article VII: Privacy, Security, and Confidentiality

Given our commitment to intellectual freedom and the sensitive nature of our discussions, absolute secrecy and security protocols govern all organizational activities.
  • All communications utilize end-to-end encryption and approved secure channels
  • Information is classified as Public, Member-Only, or Restricted based on sensitivity
  • Breach of confidentiality constitutes grounds for immediate disciplinary action
  • Members receive mandatory training in operational security practices
  • External communications about JUNTO activities are strictly prohibited without unanimous consent

Amendment Process

Article VIII: Amendments

These bylaws may be amended through a formal process requiring unanimous consent of all active members. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing at least one week prior to discussion, allowing adequate time for consideration and debate. Emergency amendments may be considered with 72-hour notice in cases affecting member security or organizational integrity.
