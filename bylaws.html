JUNTO Constitutional Framework
We, the members of JUNTO, in order to form a more perfect union of minds, establish principles of intellectual freedom, ensure the pursuit of truth, provide for common enlightenment, promote the general wisdom, and secure the blessings of consciousness to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish these Bylaws.

Article I: Name and Purpose

The organization shall be known as JUNTO, dedicated to the cultivation of enlightened individuals and the advancement of human consciousness through ethical inquiry, civic engagement, and the preservation of fundamental freedoms.
Section 1.1: Core Mission
To foster intellectual and moral development, promote active civic participation, protect privacy and security, explore esoteric knowledge, and work toward the elevation of universal consciousness.

Article II: Membership

Membership in JUNTO is by invitation and consensus, extended to individuals who demonstrate commitment to our values and possess the intellectual capacity and moral character necessary for meaningful contribution.
  • New members must be nominated by existing members and approved by consensus
  • All members enjoy equal rights and responsibilities within the organization
  • Membership may be suspended or revoked for violations of core principles
  • Members are expected to maintain confidentiality of sensitive discussions

Article III: Governance Structure

JUNTO operates under a distributed leadership model, with rotating responsibilities and collective decision-making to prevent concentration of power and ensure democratic participation.
Section 3.1: Leadership Roles
Leadership positions include Facilitator, Archivist, Guardian (Security), and Sage (Consciousness Studies). Terms are one year with possibility of renewal.
Section 3.2: Decision Making
Major decisions require consensus among all members. Minor operational matters may be decided by simple majority vote.

Article IV: Meetings and Procedures

Regular meetings shall be held monthly, with additional sessions as needed. All meetings follow established protocols to ensure productive discourse and protect member privacy.
  • Meetings may be conducted in person or through secure digital channels
  • Agenda items must be submitted 48 hours in advance when possible
  • Meeting notes are maintained but access is restricted to members only
  • Emergency meetings may be called with 24-hour notice for urgent matters

Article V: Code of Conduct

All members commit to upholding the highest standards of intellectual honesty, ethical behavior, and mutual respect in all interactions within and outside the organization.
  • Engage in good faith discourse and remain open to changing perspectives
  • Respect the privacy and confidentiality of fellow members
  • Contribute meaningfully to discussions and organizational objectives
  • Maintain the security of communication channels and sensitive information
  • Act with integrity in all matters that may reflect upon the organization

Article VI: Privacy and Security

Given our commitment to intellectual freedom and the sensitive nature of some discussions, strict protocols govern information security and member privacy protection.
Section 6.1: Information Classification
Information is classified as Public, Member-Only, or Restricted based on sensitivity and potential impact if disclosed.
Section 6.2: Communication Security
All sensitive communications utilize end-to-end encryption and approved secure channels. Members receive training in operational security practices.

Article VII: Intellectual Property and Resources

Knowledge and insights developed through collective effort remain available to all members, while respecting individual contributions and external intellectual property rights.
  • Research and analysis produced by the group is shared among members
  • Individual members retain rights to their personal contributions
  • External sources are properly attributed and copyright respected
  • The knowledge repository is collectively maintained and updated

Amendment Process

Article VIII: Amendments

These bylaws may be amended through a formal process requiring unanimous consent of all active members. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing at least one week prior to discussion, allowing adequate time for consideration and debate. Emergency amendments may be considered with 72-hour notice in cases affecting member security or organizational integrity.
